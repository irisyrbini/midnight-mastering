# Story & tone

> Source of truth for narrative intent and voice. Content is currently environmental and systemic rather than scripted; this doc governs any authored text added later.

## 1. Premise

*Make Me Happy Again* follows one music producer, alone in a small apartment studio, awake after everyone else has gone home. They are trying to finish an album. The album is real work, but it is also the thing they hide inside — a reason not to sleep, not to call anyone back, not to leave. The game is the tension between finishing the record and staying a person while doing it.

## 2. Theme

Creative obsession as both refuge and trap. The same act — working through the night — produces the album and erodes the self. Recovery is possible but never automatic: it requires deliberately choosing rest, food, contact, and the outside world over one more take.

## 3. Emotional arc

The producer begins **lonely, low on hope, love unlit** (initial crystal: red). The intended arc is not "grind to victory" but "become well enough to finish" — the album can only be completed with a **green crystal**, which demands hope, love, and reconnection. Story and mechanics say the same thing: you cannot master the record until you can bear your own company again.

## 4. Voice & register

- Second-person-adjacent, quiet, specific. Interaction descriptions are short and tender ("Play something only you need to hear.", "Look out at the city and feel less alone.").
- Never clinical, never melodramatic. No diagnosis language. No jokes at the character's expense.
- Coping objects are written without judgment but with honesty about cost ("Borrow energy from tomorrow.").

## 5. Representation guardrails (binding)

- Mental-health states are shown through light, rhythm, density, and sound-adjacent metaphor — **never** monsters, body horror, self-harm imagery, or "madness" tropes (see [ArtBible.md](ArtBible.md) §1, §7).
- No brands, no real people, no copyrighted lyrics or album art.
- Distress is quiet. A collapse ending is framed as *the night ending*, not spectacle.

## 6. Ending framing (M3)

- **Finished:** the record is done and the producer is well enough to know it — release, not triumph. Warm.
- **Collapse:** the producer runs out of tonight — they can't continue. Gentle, non-punishing; the restart is an invitation, not a game-over sting.
